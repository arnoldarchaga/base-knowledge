# iPhone Camera Basics for Truck Photography

Taking good truck photos does not mean I need an expensive camera right away. An iPhone can still take clean, sharp, and professional-looking pictures if I pay attention to the basics. The biggest thing is making sure the truck is the main focus of the photo and that the background does not distract from it. For my F-150, I would want the truck to look clean, centered, and intentional instead of just looking like a random parking lot picture.

## Focus and Composition

One simple habit is tapping the screen on the truck before taking the picture. This helps the camera focus on the subject instead of the background. I can also use the grid setting on my phone to line up the truck better. The rule of thirds can make the picture look more balanced, especially if the truck is parked at an angle.

### Quick Tips

- Clean the camera lens before taking photos.
- Avoid zooming in too much because it lowers quality.
- Take multiple shots from different angles.
- Use portrait mode carefully, not on every picture.
- Keep the truck as the clear subject.

This connects closely with [[Finding Good Locations]] because even a good camera angle can look boring if the background does not match the style of the photo.
