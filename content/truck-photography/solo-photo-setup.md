# Solo Photo Setup

One challenge with taking truck pictures is not always having someone there to take the photo. Since I do not always have a photographer, I need a setup that lets me take pictures by myself. A tripod is probably one of the most useful tools because it lets me place my phone at the right height and angle. Even a simple phone tripod can make the photos look more planned.

## Using a Timer

The iPhone timer is helpful for solo shots. I can set the phone on a tripod, choose the angle, start the timer, and then walk into the frame if I want to be in the picture. This would work well if I want photos standing beside the truck or leaning against it. I can also take photos without myself in them and just focus on the truck.

### Helpful Gear

- Phone tripod
- Bluetooth camera remote
- Microfiber cloth for cleaning the lens
- Portable light for night photos
- Extra phone storage or cloud backup

Solo photos require patience because I might need to retake the same shot several times. This connects to [[Finding Good Locations]] because I need safe, open places where I can set up a tripod without people or cars getting in the way.
