# Focus and Exposure


Getting the focus and exposure right is important because the truck should look clear without being too dark or too bright. My iPhone normally handles these settings automatically, but I can still adjust them before taking the picture. I always check the screen carefully because reflections from the paint, windows, and chrome can affect how the camera reads the lighting.

## Selecting the Focus Point

Before taking a picture, I tap directly on the truck on my screen. For a front photograph, I usually tap near the grille or headlights. For a side photograph, I tap near the doors or front wheel. This tells the phone that the truck should be the sharpest part of the image.

If I tap the background by mistake, the truck may appear slightly blurry. This is especially noticeable in close-up pictures like the ones described on the [[composition/detail-shots|Detail Shots]] page.

## Adjusting the Exposure

After tapping the screen, a small sun symbol appears next to the focus box. I can move my finger up to brighten the picture or down to make it darker. If sunlight is reflecting strongly from the hood or windshield, I lower the exposure slightly.

I try not to make the photograph too dark because that can hide the truck’s body lines and wheel details. The goal is to keep the paint visible while avoiding bright areas that lose their detail.

## Locking Focus and Exposure

If I press and hold the screen, my iPhone can lock the focus and exposure. This can help when I want to take several pictures from the same position without the brightness changing every time.

## Helpful Tips

- Tap directly on the truck before taking the picture.
- Check that the grille, headlights, and wheels look sharp.
- Lower the exposure when the paint looks too bright.
- Avoid making the shadows completely black.
- Review the picture before changing camera positions.
- Take several versions with slightly different exposure levels.
