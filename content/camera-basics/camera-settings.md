# Camera Settings

Using the right camera settings helps me take clearer and better organized pictures of my F-150. I do not need to change every setting on my iPhone, but a few simple adjustments can make a noticeable difference. I normally check these settings before leaving for a photo shoot so I do not have to fix everything once I arrive at the location.

## Turn On the Camera Grid

One of the most useful settings is the camera grid. The grid places lines across the screen that help me keep the truck and the horizon straight. It also helps me use the ideas explained on the [[composition/rule-of-thirds|Rule of Thirds]] page.

When using the grid, I try to avoid placing the truck in a random position. I can line up the horizon with one of the horizontal grid lines and make sure the truck has enough space around it.

## Focus and Brightness

Before taking a picture, I tap the truck on the screen so the camera focuses on it. I normally tap near the grille, headlights, or side of the truck. After tapping the screen, I can adjust the brightness by moving the small sun symbol up or down.

If the truck’s paint or windshield looks too bright, I lower the exposure slightly. More information about controlling these settings is included on the [[camera-basics/focus-and-exposure|Focus and Exposure]] page.

## Flash and Live Photos

I normally keep the flash turned off when taking pictures during the day. The flash is usually not strong enough to light an entire truck and may create unwanted reflections. At night, I still prefer using available lighting and a steady tripod instead of depending only on the flash.

Live Photos can be helpful because they capture a small amount of movement before and after the picture. However, I still take several regular pictures from each angle so I have more choices later.

## Review the Photograph

After taking a picture, I zoom in and check the grille, headlights, wheels, and body lines. I also check the background for distracting objects. This saves time because I can correct mistakes before leaving the location.

## Helpful Tips

- Turn on the camera grid before the photo shoot.
- Tap the truck on the screen to focus.
- Lower the exposure when the paint looks too bright.
- Keep the flash off during most daytime pictures.
- Take several versions of each photograph.
- Review the pictures before leaving the location.
