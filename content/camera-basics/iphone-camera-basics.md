# iPhone Camera Basics

An iPhone can take good truck photographs without needing an expensive professional camera. The most important part is understanding a few basic camera features and taking time to set up the picture correctly. I use my iPhone because it is convenient, easy to carry, and capable of producing clear photographs of my F-150.

## Clean the Camera Lens

Before taking any pictures, I clean the camera lens with a soft cloth. Since my phone stays in my pocket, the lens can collect fingerprints, dust, and dirt. A dirty lens can make the picture look blurry or create unwanted glare around lights.

## Choose the Correct Lens

For most full-truck photographs, I use the regular `1x` camera lens. This lens usually keeps the truck looking natural without stretching its shape. The wide-angle lens can be useful when I am standing in a tight space, but it may make the front or rear of the truck look larger than it really is.

I try to avoid using digital zoom because it can lower the quality of the image. Instead, I move closer to the truck when possible.

## Keep the Phone Steady

I hold the phone with both hands and pause before pressing the shutter button. If I am taking pictures by myself, I can use the methods explained on the [[planning/solo-photo-setup|Solo Photo Setup]] page.

## Take Multiple Pictures

I do not depend on only one photograph. I take several pictures from slightly different positions and review them before leaving the location. This gives me more options when I begin the [[editing-and-sharing/iphone-editing-workflow|iPhone Editing Workflow]].

## Helpful Tips

- Clean the camera lens before every photo shoot.
    
- Use the `1x` lens for most full-truck pictures.
    
- Avoid using too much digital zoom.
    
- Hold the phone with both hands.
    
- Take several versions of each angle.
    
- Check the pictures before leaving the location.
