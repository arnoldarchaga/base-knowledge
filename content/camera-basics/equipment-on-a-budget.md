# Equipment on a Budget

Good truck photography does not require a professional camera or expensive equipment. Since I mainly use my iPhone, I can build a simple setup with a few affordable items. These tools make it easier to keep the phone steady, take pictures by myself, and keep the truck looking clean during the photo shoot.

## Phone Tripod

A basic phone tripod is one of the most useful items I can buy. It keeps the phone steady and allows me to take pictures without holding it. A tripod is especially helpful for [[lighting/night-photography|Night Photography]] because even a small amount of movement can make a dark photograph look blurry.

The tripod does not need to be expensive. It only needs to hold the phone securely and be tall enough for the angles I want.

## Bluetooth Camera Remote

A small Bluetooth remote allows me to take pictures while standing away from the phone. This is useful when I want to include myself in a photograph or avoid touching the phone after setting up the shot. I can also use the camera timer, but the remote gives me more control.

More information about using both tools is included on the [[planning/solo-photo-setup|Solo Photo Setup]] page.

## Cleaning Supplies

I also bring microfiber towels, glass cleaner, and a lens cloth. The towels help me remove dust, fingerprints, and water spots from the truck. The lens cloth keeps my iPhone camera clear.

## Optional Lighting

A small rechargeable light can help with close-up pictures at night. I would not expect it to light the entire truck, but it can help show badges, wheels, or interior details.

## Budget Equipment List

- Phone tripod
- Bluetooth camera remote
- Microfiber towels
- Camera lens cloth
- Glass cleaner
- Small rechargeable light
- Portable phone charger

The most important equipment is still the phone I already own. Learning how to use it correctly matters more than buying unnecessary accessories.
