# iPhone Editing Workflow
Using the same editing process helps me avoid making random changes to every photograph. I can edit directly in the iPhone Photos app or use another photo-editing application. The exact numbers may change from picture to picture, but I follow the same general order.

## Select the Best Pictures

I begin by reviewing all the photographs and marking the strongest ones as favorites. I remove pictures that are blurry, badly framed, or almost identical to a better version. This keeps me from wasting time editing every picture.

## Crop and Straighten

Before adjusting the lighting, I crop the photograph and straighten the horizon. I make sure the tires, bumper, mirrors, and other important parts of the truck are not accidentally cut off.

I also use ideas from the [[composition/rule-of-thirds|Rule of Thirds]] page when deciding how much space to leave around the truck.

## Adjust the Lighting

I make small changes to exposure and brightness. Then I lower the highlights if the sky, hood, or windshield is too bright. I raise the shadows slightly if the grille, wheels, or lower body are too dark.

## Adjust the Appearance

After correcting the lighting, I adjust contrast, black point, saturation, warmth, definition, and sharpness only when needed. I avoid moving every setting to a high level because that can make the picture look unnatural.

## Review the Final Image

I compare the edited version with the original and zoom in to check the details. I also view the photograph at normal size to see how it will look when posted.

## Editing Order

1. Select the best photograph.
2. Crop and straighten it.
3. Adjust exposure.
4. Lower highlights.
5. Raise shadows carefully.
6. Adjust contrast and color.
7. Add a small amount of sharpness.
8. Review and save the final version.
