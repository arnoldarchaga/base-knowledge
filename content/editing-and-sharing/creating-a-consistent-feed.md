# Creating a Consistent Feed
A consistent Instagram feed does not mean every photograph has to look exactly the same. It means the pictures should feel connected through similar editing, lighting, subjects, and organization. Since my main subject is my F-150, I can use different locations and angles while still maintaining a recognizable style.

## Use Similar Editing

I try to keep the brightness, contrast, color, and overall mood similar across several posts. If one picture has a natural style and the next one uses an extreme filter, the feed may look disconnected.

The editing choices described on the [[editing-and-sharing/editing-and-instagram-style|Editing and Instagram Style]] page help keep the truck’s appearance realistic.

## Mix Different Types of Pictures

Posting only the same front angle can become repetitive. I can alternate between full-truck photographs, rear angles, side profiles, and close-up details. I may also include occasional pictures of myself with the truck when I use the [[planning/solo-photo-setup|Solo Photo Setup]].

## Plan the Order

Before posting, I look at my recent pictures and decide which one fits best next. I avoid posting several nearly identical images in a row. I can save pictures in a separate album so I know which ones are ready to post.

## Keep the Feed Focused

I do not have to remove every personal picture, but I try to avoid filling the feed with unrelated screenshots or low-quality images. Stories can be used for temporary content that does not match the main feed.

## Helpful Tips

- Use similar editing across several photographs.
- Alternate full-truck and detail pictures.
- Avoid posting the same angle repeatedly.
- Save finished pictures in a separate album.
- Review the feed before selecting the next post.
- Develop a style that still feels natural.
