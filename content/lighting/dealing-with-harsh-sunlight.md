# Dealing with Harsh Sunlight
Strong sunlight can make truck photography difficult because it creates bright reflections and deep shadows. The hood, windshield, chrome, and windows may become overly bright while the grille and areas underneath the truck appear too dark. When possible, I avoid photographing during the strongest part of the day, but sometimes I still need to work with those conditions.

## Look for Open Shade

One option is to park in open shade created by a large building or structure. Open shade reduces direct sunlight while still providing enough light to show the truck clearly. I avoid parking under small trees because the leaves may create uneven spots of light across the paint.

## Change the Camera Angle

If the hood or windshield has a strong reflection, I move to another side of the truck. Even a small change in position can affect where the sunlight appears. I also check the direction of the sun before deciding how to park.

The different camera positions described on the [[composition/angles-for-trucks|Angles for Trucks]] page can help me find a better view.

## Lower the Exposure

I tap on the truck and move the exposure control downward if the brightest areas are losing detail. I make small adjustments because lowering it too much can make the shadows too dark.

## Edit Carefully

During editing, I can lower the highlights and slightly raise the shadows. However, editing cannot completely fix a photograph where large areas are completely white or black. It is better to control the light while taking the picture.

## Helpful Tips

- Avoid midday photo shoots when possible.
- Use open shade instead of uneven tree shade.
- Change the camera or truck position.
- Lower the exposure slightly.
- Watch the windshield and hood for reflections.
- Take several test pictures before continuing.
