# Golden Hour
Golden hour is the short period after sunrise or before sunset when the sunlight appears softer and warmer. This is one of the best times for me to photograph my F-150 because the light is usually less harsh than it is during the middle of the day.

## Why Golden Hour Works

The lower sun can create longer shadows that help show the truck’s shape and body lines. It can also give the paint a warmer appearance. Chrome, windows, and headlights may still reflect the sunlight, but the reflections are often easier to control.

Golden hour does not last very long, so I need to arrive prepared. I use the [[planning/photo-shoot-checklist|Photo Shoot Checklist]] before leaving home and try to have the truck clean before reaching the location.

## Positioning the Truck

I experiment with placing the sunlight beside or slightly behind the truck. Side lighting can show the body lines, while backlighting can create a bright outline around the vehicle. If the front of the truck becomes too dark, I change the angle or slightly raise the exposure.

I avoid pointing the camera directly at the sun unless I want a strong glare effect. Moving a few steps can reduce unwanted lens flare.

## Planning the Session

Because the light changes quickly, I begin with the most important full-truck angles. After that, I take side profiles and [[composition/detail-shots|Detail Shots]]. I review a few pictures during the session, but I avoid spending too much time looking at every image.

## Helpful Tips

- Arrive before the best light begins.
- Clean the truck before reaching the location.
- Start with the most important angles.
- Try lighting from both sides.
- Watch for glare and lens flare.
- Take several pictures as the light changes.
