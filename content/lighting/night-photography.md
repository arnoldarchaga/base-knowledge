# Night Photography
Night photography can create a darker and more dramatic style, but it is more difficult than taking pictures during the day. The phone needs enough light to show the truck clearly, and even a small movement can make the picture blurry. I take extra time to choose the location and keep the phone steady.

## Choosing a Lighted Location

I look for areas with useful lighting, such as parking garages, gas stations, downtown streets, or parking lots with large lights. The location should be bright enough to show the truck without creating extreme glare.

I avoid places that are completely dark because the phone may struggle to capture enough detail. Safety is also important, so I choose legal locations where I am allowed to park.

## Keeping the Phone Steady

A tripod is extremely useful at night. It prevents movement and allows the phone to use a longer exposure when necessary. I can use a timer or Bluetooth remote so I do not shake the phone while pressing the button.

These tools are also described on the [[camera-basics/equipment-on-a-budget|Equipment on a Budget]] page.

## Controlling Reflections

Streetlights can create bright reflections on the hood, windshield, and windows. I move the truck or camera slightly until the reflections look controlled. I also check for glare around lights, which can become worse if the camera lens is dirty.

## Reviewing the Pictures

I zoom in after taking each important angle. A picture can look clear on the small screen but still be blurry when viewed closely.

## Helpful Tips

- Clean the camera lens before starting.
- Use a tripod whenever possible.
- Choose a location with useful lighting.
- Avoid depending only on the phone flash.
- Take several versions of each photograph.
- Check the pictures for blur before leaving.
