# Background Selection
The background is almost as important as the truck itself. A clean background helps the truck stand out, while a crowded background can distract from it. Before taking a picture, I look beyond the truck and check everything that will appear in the frame.

## Avoiding Distractions

I try to avoid backgrounds with too many parked cars, people, trash cans, signs, or shopping carts. I also check for poles, trees, and light fixtures that may appear to come directly out of the roof or bed of the truck.

Sometimes I only need to move the truck a few feet or change my camera angle to remove a distracting object.

## Matching the Background to the Truck

Different locations can create different styles. An industrial area can give the truck a tougher appearance, while an open field or park can create a cleaner and more relaxed photograph. Downtown buildings can work well when I want a more modern look.

The location should support the appearance I want without becoming more noticeable than the truck. Ideas for choosing locations are included on the [[planning/finding-good-locations|Finding Good Locations]] page.

## Checking the Ground

I also check the ground around the truck. Trash, oil stains, deep cracks, and large puddles can distract from the photograph. A clean parking area usually makes editing easier.

## Final Frame Check

Before pressing the shutter button, I scan the entire screen from one corner to the other. I check the sky, ground, sides, and area behind the truck.

## Helpful Tips

- Choose simple backgrounds whenever possible.
- Avoid crowded parking lots.
- Check for poles behind the truck.
- Remove visible trash when it is safe to do so.
- Make sure the horizon is straight.
- Take test photographs before settling on one position.
