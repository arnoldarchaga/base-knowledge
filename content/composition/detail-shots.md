# Detail Shots
Full-truck photographs show the overall vehicle, but detail shots help show smaller features that may be missed from a distance. These pictures can include the wheels, headlights, grille, badges, interior, or other parts that make the truck unique. I can use detail shots between full-truck pictures to add variety to my collection or Instagram feed.

## Exterior Details

The wheels and tires are good subjects because they can show the style and stance of the truck. I can also photograph the headlights, taillights, grille, emblems, mirrors, door handles, and tailgate.

When taking these pictures, I move close enough to fill most of the frame without using too much digital zoom. I tap directly on the detail so the phone focuses on it, using the techniques from [[camera-basics/focus-and-exposure|Focus and Exposure]].

## Interior Details

Interior detail shots can include the steering wheel, dashboard, seats, center console, or instrument panel. Before taking these photographs, I remove trash and personal items that could make the interior look messy.

I also watch for reflections in the screen and windows. Changing my position slightly can sometimes remove the reflection.

## Lighting for Close-Ups

Soft lighting usually works better than strong direct sunlight. Harsh light can create bright reflections on chrome, glass, and painted surfaces. During a night photo shoot, a small portable light can help reveal details without lighting the entire truck.

## Helpful Tips

- Clean the area before photographing it.
- Move closer instead of using digital zoom.
- Tap the detail on the screen to focus.
- Try both horizontal and vertical photographs.
- Watch for reflections in chrome and glass.
- Use detail shots with full-truck pictures.
