# Rule of Thirds
The rule of thirds is a simple way to organize a photograph. When the camera grid is turned on, the screen is divided into nine sections using two horizontal lines and two vertical lines. These lines help me decide where to position the truck, the horizon, and the open space around it.

## Positioning the Truck

Instead of placing the truck directly in the center every time, I can position it closer to one of the vertical grid lines. This creates space in the direction the truck is facing and can make the photograph look more balanced.

For example, if the front of the truck is pointing toward the right side of the picture, I can place the truck slightly to the left. This leaves open space in front of it instead of making it look crowded against the edge.

## Keeping the Horizon Straight

The horizontal grid lines also help me keep the horizon straight. A tilted parking lot, building, or skyline can make the entire photograph feel unbalanced. I line up the horizon or another straight background feature with one of the horizontal lines.

The grid setting is explained further on the [[camera-basics/camera-settings|Camera Settings]] page.

## When Centering Works

The rule of thirds is not a strict rule that must be followed in every picture. A centered photograph can work well when the background is symmetrical or when I want the front grille to be the main focus. The important part is making the placement look intentional.

## Helpful Tips

- Turn on the camera grid before the photo shoot.
- Leave space in front of the truck.
- Use the horizontal lines to straighten the horizon.
- Avoid placing the truck too close to an edge.
- Compare centered and off-center versions.
- Check the full frame before taking the picture.
