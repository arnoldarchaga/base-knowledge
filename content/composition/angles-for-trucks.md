# Angles for Trucks
The angle of the camera can completely change how a truck looks in a photograph. Some angles make the truck appear wider, taller, or more aggressive, while others can make its shape look awkward. I try several different positions instead of standing in one place and taking every picture from eye level.

## Front Three-Quarter Angle

The front three-quarter angle shows the front of the truck and one complete side. I usually stand near one of the front corners and move back until the entire truck fits in the frame. This angle works well because it shows the grille, headlights, wheels, and body lines at the same time.

I avoid standing too close with the wide-angle lens because it can make the front of the truck look much larger than the rear.

## Rear Three-Quarter Angle

The rear three-quarter angle is similar, but it shows the tailgate, taillights, bed, and one side. This is useful when the rear of the truck has details I want to show, such as the exhaust, bumper, or tailgate design.

## Side Profile

A side profile photograph shows the truck directly from the side. I try to keep the phone level and make sure the wheels are not cut off. This angle is good for showing the truck’s overall length, ride height, and wheel setup.

## Lower Camera Angles

Holding the phone slightly lower can make the truck appear taller and stronger. However, placing the phone too close to the ground may create an unnatural view. I usually experiment with different heights and compare the results later.

## Helpful Tips

- Try both front corners of the truck.
- Take rear three-quarter photographs from both sides.
- Keep the entire truck inside the frame.
- Avoid cutting off the tires or bumper.
- Step back instead of depending on digital zoom.
- Check the [[composition/background-selection|Background Selection]] before taking the picture.
