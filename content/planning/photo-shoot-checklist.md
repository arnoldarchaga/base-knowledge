# Photo Shoot Checklist
A checklist helps me remember the important steps before, during, and after a truck photo shoot. It is easy to forget equipment or leave the location without taking an important angle. I can review this page before leaving home and again before ending the session.

## Before Leaving Home

- [ ] Charge my iPhone.
- [ ] Check the available storage space.
- [ ] Clean the camera lens.
- [ ] Wash and dry the truck.
- [ ] Clean the wheels, windows, and mirrors.
- [ ] Remove trash from the interior.
- [ ] Pack the phone tripod.
- [ ] Pack the Bluetooth remote.
- [ ] Bring microfiber towels and cleaner.
- [ ] Check the weather.
- [ ] Confirm the location.
- [ ] Choose a backup location.

## At the Location

- [ ] Check that parking is allowed.
- [ ] Look at the direction of the light.
- [ ] Check the full background.
- [ ] Remove small distractions when it is safe.
- [ ] Take a test photograph.
- [ ] Adjust focus and exposure.
- [ ] Take front three-quarter photographs.
- [ ] Take rear three-quarter photographs.
- [ ] Take a side profile.
- [ ] Take close-up detail shots.
- [ ] Try vertical and horizontal pictures.
- [ ] Take several versions of each angle.

## Before Leaving the Location

- [ ] Review the pictures closely.
- [ ] Check for blurry photographs.
- [ ] Confirm that no part of the truck is cut off.
- [ ] Check the background for distractions.
- [ ] Retake any missing angles.
- [ ] Collect the tripod and other equipment.
- [ ] Check the area for personal items or trash.

This checklist works with the planning methods on the [[planning/finding-good-locations|Finding Good Locations]] and [[planning/preparing-the-truck|Preparing the Truck]] pages.
