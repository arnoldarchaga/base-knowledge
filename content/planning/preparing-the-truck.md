# Preparing the Truck
Preparing the truck before a photo shoot saves time and improves the final pictures. Dirt, fingerprints, water spots, and trash can become more noticeable in photographs than they appear in person. I try to clean the important areas before leaving home and bring basic supplies for last-minute corrections.

## Wash the Exterior

I wash the body of the truck and remove visible dirt from the paint. I pay extra attention to the grille, headlights, taillights, mirrors, and lower panels. Water spots can show up under strong sunlight, so I dry the truck instead of allowing it to air-dry.

## Clean the Wheels and Tires

The wheels and tires are noticeable in full-truck photographs and close-up pictures. I remove brake dust and dirt from the wheels and wipe the tires. I avoid applying too much tire shine because it can look overly reflective or uneven in pictures.

## Check the Windows and Interior

I clean the windshield and windows on both sides when possible. I also remove trash, bottles, jackets, and other items from the interior. If I plan to take interior photographs, I organize the seats, dashboard, and center console.

## Remove Distracting Items

I remove unnecessary objects from the truck bed and make sure straps, tools, or other items are not hanging from the vehicle. At the location, I do a final walk around the truck before taking pictures.

## Supplies to Bring

- Microfiber towels
- Glass cleaner
- Lens cloth
- Tire towel
- Water
- Trash bag
- Phone charger

Preparing the truck also makes the [[composition/detail-shots|Detail Shots]] look cleaner and reduces the amount of editing needed later.
